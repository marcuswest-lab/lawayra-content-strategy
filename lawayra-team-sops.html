Section 02

Social Media Approach

Filming guidelines, consent protocol, and creative direction for capturing authentic content at LaWayra.

Why We Share

At LaWayra, social media is about sharing the beauty, energy, and healing. Our goals: guests keep beautiful memories, friends and family feel the experience, and future guests feel inspired to begin their own journey.

⚠ Consent First — Non-Negotiable

Head Facilitator asks guests during tour if they are okay being filmed.

Anyone who opts out will NOT be recorded under any circumstance.

Know who not to include before filming begins.

What to Capture

Before & After Ceremony

  • Arrival bus and guest welcome
  • Opening circle, musical intros, team greetings
  • Ceremony prep: meditation, Taita preparing smoke, altar, instruments
  • Closing ceremonies or integration circles (without filming sensitive moments)
🚫 Important

Only film Taita, smoke, and general ambiance during ceremony prep — never guests in process.

Daily Activities

  • Yoga sessions, clay baths, hangout areas
  • Meals being shared, landscapes, quiet moments
  • Staff and volunteer interactions

Vibe & Style Direction

  • Show healing energy, natural beauty, authentic connections
  • Think quiet, calm, cozy, and connected — not flashy or intrusive
  • Soft light, kind smiles, heartfelt music, lush green surroundings
🎬 End-of-Week Reel

Have the reel ready by Saturday night.

Sunday when guests leave: share with them, offer a gift (t-shirt or bracelet) if they post and tag LaWayra.

Section 03

Taking Testimonials

A step-by-step SOP for capturing authentic, heartfelt video testimonials every Sunday after retreat ends.

When & Where

  • Timing: Sunday after retreat ends
  • Location: Quiet space, good lighting, calm background
  • Equipment: Phone on tripod, portrait mode (optimized for Reels)
  • Duration: 2–3 minutes max
  • Lighting: Well-lit face — natural light is best
  • Incentive: LaWayra bracelet or T-shirt

Before Filming — Consent + Briefing

  1. Ask if they'd like to share a video testimonial
  2. Explain: "To help others who are feeling called but unsure or scared"
  3. Let them know they can pause, redo, or say no at any time
  4. Clarify it may be posted on Instagram or the website

Suggested Questions

1
"What has your experience at LaWayra been like?"
2
"Did you come with an intention? How did that evolve?"
3
"What was the ceremony experience like for you?"
4
"How did you feel before coming to LaWayra?"
5
"And how do you feel now, as you're leaving?"

Filming Tips

  • Encourage slow, heartfelt speaking
  • Stay emotionally attuned — this is vulnerable
  • Give space if someone tears up
  • Smile, listen, offer grounding energy
  • Label videos clearly (e.g., Emma_Testimonial_Sunday_03/30/2026.mp4)

Words to Avoid

Shadowban prevention guide. Use safer alternatives to protect reach and stay within community guidelines.

Risky Words → Safer Alternatives

🚫 Risky Word ✅ Safer Alternative
AyahuascaSacred brew / ancestral tradition
PsychedelicsConsciousness-expanding practices
TripInner journey
Heal traumaProcess old wounds / release stuck energy
CureSupport healing / promote balance
ShamanGuide / facilitator / medicine leader
DrugSacred plant / traditional remedy
HallucinateExperience visions / symbolic imagery
AddictionUnhealthy patterns / cycles
Mental illnessEmotional challenges

Hashtags

Risky — Avoid These

#psychedelics #ayahuasca #plantmedicine #healingtrauma #spiritualawakening #lsd #dmt #mushrooms

Safer Alternatives

#sacredjourney #innerwork #soulretreat #healingpath

Best Practices

  • Rotate hashtags weekly — never repeat the same set
  • Avoid too many spiritual or medicine hashtags in a single post
  • Keep captions human and natural
  • Never promise outcomes or make medical claims
  • Prioritize community guidelines language over industry jargon

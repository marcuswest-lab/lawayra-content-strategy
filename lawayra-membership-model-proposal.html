Word of mouth is already LaWayra's second-largest booking source (64 of 266 tracked bookings). That's a community wanting to exist — they just don't have a container for it yet. Every guest who leaves wanting to come back but forgets is lost lifetime value. Every past guest who recommends LaWayra to a friend is doing free marketing.

Pattern

The winning models all share one trait: they deliver value between the anchor experience. Peloton between rides. Mindvalley between events. Nectara between ceremonies. The membership that only gives a discount on the next retreat will fail — the membership that fills the 364 days in between will thrive.

Pricing Rationale

Why $29/mo for Familia

Nectara charges $20/mo. Mindvalley charges $49/mo. At $29, LaWayra sits in the sweet spot — premium enough to signal value, affordable enough for a global audience. At $348/year, it's less than a single retreat ($545+), making the retreat discount alone nearly worth the cost for anyone planning to return.

Why $97/mo for Raices

The 1-on-1 coaching session alone would cost $100-200 on the open market. Members who want deep personal support are already paying for therapists and coaches. This packages LaWayra's unique perspective (ceremony integration, spiritual guidance) at a fraction of standalone coaching costs.

Why Free for 3 Months

Soltara bundles a full year of Nectara free. Temple of the Way of Light gives 3 months free. The post-retreat window is when guests are most vulnerable, most grateful, and most likely to convert. A free period proves value before asking for payment — and prevents early churn.

The Gym Membership Psychology

Sam's instinct is backed by data: gym membership retention is driven by identity, not usage. People who call themselves "members" behave differently than "customers." LaWayra Familia isn't a purchase — it's a belonging. The monthly payment is a commitment to their own healing journey.

05 — Roadmap

How to Get There

A phased approach. Start simple, prove value, then build the full ecosystem.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Launch the Free Integration Program
Start with what costs nothing and proves everything. Every departing guest gets enrolled automatically.
  • Set up a private community space (Circle app recommended — $39/mo for the platform)
  • Create 12-week email integration series (repurpose existing content + shaman teachings)
  • Host 2 group integration calls per month (facilitators volunteer, record for library)
  • Build the integration resource library (meditation recordings, journaling prompts, diet guidance)
  • Collect feedback and testimonials from participants
Month 1-3
Phase 2 — Activation
Launch Familia Paid Tier + Alumni Outreach
Once the free community has 200+ members and proven engagement, introduce the paid tier.
  • Email the 2,500+ alumni list: "LaWayra Familia is here. You're already part of the family — here's how to stay connected."
  • Launch weekly live calls (integration circle + breathwork + guest speakers on rotation)
  • Activate crisis support line (train 3-5 volunteers, create protocol)
  • Start producing member-only content (Sam's voice notes/teachings are perfect raw material)
  • Offer founding member pricing: $19/mo for first 100 members (locked in forever)
Month 4-6

With a thriving Familia community, add the premium tier and expand offerings.

  • Develop facilitator training pathway (long-term revenue, deepens community)
  • Explore extended-stay program for Raices members (Sam's "you can even get to live there" vision)
  • Build podcast or video series featuring community stories (content flywheel)

  • Eventually: the retreat offering itself becomes just one feature of a larger membership

What You Need to Build It

Minimal tech stack. Most of it plugs into what LaWayra already has.

Community Platform: Circle

$39-89/mo. Used by Nectara, Mindvalley, and hundreds of membership businesses. Built-in spaces, events, courses, live rooms, and mobile app. Members can access from anywhere. Integrates with Zapier for automation.

Payment: Stripe + Circle

Circle has built-in paid membership tiers with Stripe integration. Members pay through the platform. No separate billing system needed. Handles free trials, upgrades, cancellations automatically.

Email: Existing Tools

The 12-week integration series can run through whatever email tool LaWayra uses. Triggered automatically when a guest checks out of BookingLayer. n8n can handle the automation if needed.

Circle has built-in live rooms for community calls. For larger events or guest speakers, Zoom works fine. Recordings go into the content library automatically.

Content Library

Hosted within Circle as "Spaces." Meditation recordings, integration guides, ceremony prep content, shaman teachings. Members access everything in one place via the Circle app.

Automation: n8n + Zapier

Already have both running (n8n at server.lawayraserver.com). BookingLayer checkout → auto-enroll in Circle community. Zapier handles payment events. n8n handles content + notifications.

07 — Risks & Considerations

Eyes Wide Open

Every model has risks. Here's how to navigate them.

⚠
The Cold Start Problem
A community with 5 people on a Zoom call feels empty. A community with 50 feels alive. The early months are the hardest — you need critical mass before the community becomes self-sustaining.
Mitigation: Start with the free tier to build numbers. Offer founding member pricing to incentivize early adopters. Personally invite the most engaged past guests. 2,500+ alumni is a large base to seed from.
⚠
Cannibalization Concern
Sam asked: "If they pay $100/month and get 1-3 retreats a year, does that reduce per-retreat revenue?" Valid concern. At $1,200/year for a retreat that costs $545+, the math needs to work.
Mitigation: The proposed model doesn't include free retreats. It offers discounts (15-25%) that still price above cost. The revenue gain from members who wouldn't have rebooked at all far outweighs the discount given to those who would have. Net positive.
⚠
Content & Facilitation Bandwidth
Weekly calls, crisis support, content creation — someone has to do the work. If it falls on Sam or the core team already running retreats, it'll burn out fast.
Mitigation: Phase 1 uses existing resources (facilitators, recorded content). Phase 2 recruits trained alumni as volunteer facilitators. Phase 3's Raices revenue funds paid community managers. The community should eventually sustain its own labor.
⚠
Ethical Sensitivity
Sam himself raised this: "I don't know how ethical it is." Monetizing a sacred healing experience through a subscription model could feel transactional if not handled with care. The ayahuasca community is sensitive to commercialization.
Mitigation: Frame it as ongoing support, not a product. The free integration tier demonstrates care comes first, payment comes later. Crisis support is a genuine need, not a sales tactic. Let the community name and shape itself — don't over-brand it.
⚠
Churn After Initial Excitement
Many membership businesses see high initial sign-ups followed by 3-6 month churn. If members don't find ongoing value, they'll cancel after the novelty wears off.
Mitigation: Multiple engagement touchpoints (Peloton's lesson: multi-discipline users churn 60% less). Vary the content: integration calls, breathwork, guest speakers, shaman teachings, community challenges. Make the annual reunion a can't-miss event. Track engagement weekly and adapt.

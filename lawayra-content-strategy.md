## 1. Content Pillars

Four pillars that cover the full buyer journey — from "never heard of ayahuasca" to "ready to book."

### Pillar 1: Educate & Warn
**Purpose:** Reach new audiences, build authority, go viral
**Frequency:** 1-2x per week
**Primary Format:** Reels (face-to-camera + bold text overlay)
**Awareness Levels:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware

This is LaWayra's growth engine. Research shows warning/educational Reels get 10-20x more views than any other content type. Nico Canon's warning hooks hit 393K views on a 19.8K account.

**Example Topics:**
- "Watch this before your first ayahuasca ceremony"
- "How ayahuasca actually works in the brain"
- "The difference between authentic and tourist ayahuasca"
- "Why talk therapy alone isn't enough for some people"
- "3 red flags that expose a fake ayahuasca retreat"

**Content Structure:**
- Seconds 0-3: Bold text overlay + face + verbal hook
- Seconds 3-25: Teach one clear concept (the "meat")
- Seconds 25-30: Value payoff + soft CTA ("Save this for when you're ready")
- Caption: Expand the lesson + "Link in bio to start your journey"

---

### Pillar 2: Show & Immerse
**Purpose:** Let people experience the retreat before booking, reduce uncertainty
**Frequency:** 1x per week
**Primary Format:** Reels (POV tours, cinematic B-roll, day-in-the-life)
**Awareness Levels:** Solution Aware → Product Aware

POV facility tours hit 220K views for a competitor with 49K followers. This format has the highest viral ceiling with the lowest creative effort — just walk and film.

**Example Topics:**
- POV walkthrough: arrival → room → ceremony space → meals → nature
- Day in the life at LaWayra (sunrise to sunset)
- The food at LaWayra (meal prep, ingredients, presentation)
- The ceremony space at night (atmospheric, low-light)
- Fredonia and the surrounding mountains

**Content Structure:**
- Seconds 0-2: Bold text overlay ("Come inside Colombia's #1 ayahuasca retreat")
- Seconds 2-30: Smooth walking POV footage with trending audio
- Caption: Describe the experience + booking CTA
- Add trending audio for discoverability

---

### Pillar 3: Prove & Trust
**Purpose:** Convert consideration into bookings through social proof
**Frequency:** 1x per week
**Primary Format:** Reels (testimonial compilations) + Carousels (review graphics)
**Awareness Levels:** Product Aware → Most Aware

LaWayra's 500+ 5-star reviews and 2,500+ lives transformed are massively underused. Research shows "parade of proof" compilations get 10-15K views, while plain single testimonials only hit 2-4K. The key: stack multiple voices fast with bold text.

**Example Topics:**
- "What people are saying about LaWayra" (rapid-fire review clips)
- Guest before/after emotional transformation (with permission)
- "Why 2,500+ people chose LaWayra" (review quote graphics carousel)
- DM screenshots: real messages from past guests
- "Our shaman has guided 2,500+ ceremonies — here's his philosophy"

**Content Structure:**
- Seconds 0-2: Bold hook ("500+ 5-star reviews. Here's what they said.")
- Seconds 2-25: Quick cuts of 3-5 different guest clips OR review quotes on screen
- Seconds 25-30: CTA ("Ready to write your own story? Link in bio")
- Caption: Feature one long-form guest testimonial quote

---

### Pillar 4: Story & Connect
**Purpose:** Humanize the brand, build emotional connection, retain followers
**Frequency:** Stories daily when possible, 1 Reel per 2 weeks
**Primary Format:** Stories (behind-the-scenes, day-to-day), occasional Reel
**Awareness Levels:** All levels (deepens relationship at every stage)

This is the "10x" in the 10:1 give-to-ask ratio. Casual, unpolished, human content that makes followers feel like insiders. No bold text overlays needed — this is raw and real.

**Example Topics:**
- The shaman's morning ritual
- Preparing the ceremony space
- Sunset from the retreat center
- "Why we started LaWayra" (origin story Reel)
- Answering a common DM question in Stories
- Quick "ask me anything" Story poll

**Content Structure:**
- Stories: Casual, phone-filmed, no editing needed. Just share the moment.
- Reels: When a story is powerful enough, give it the full hook-meat-reward treatment
- Caption: Personal, conversational tone

## 3. Content Assembly System (Solo Creator Workflow)

### Weekly Batch Filming Protocol

**One 2-3 hour filming session per week produces all content for the next 7 days.**

**Pre-Film (30 min):**
1. Pick 4 hooks from the Hook Bank above
2. Write bullet points for each "meat" section (3-5 key points)
3. Set up filming location (good natural light, jungle/nature in background)
4. Phone on tripod, or handheld for POV content

**Film Session (90 min):**
| Order | What to Film | Time |
|-------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Face-to-camera hooks (film all 4 hooks back-to-back) | 30 min |
| 2 | Full meat sections for 2-3 Reels | 30 min |
| 3 | POV walkthrough footage (if on schedule) | 15 min |
| 4 | B-roll: nature, ceremony space, food, details | 15 min |

**Post-Film (60 min):**
- Edit 4 Reels in CapCut or Instagram editor
- Add bold text overlays (see Visual Standards below)
- Write captions using the Caption Template
- Schedule for the week (or save as drafts)

**Pro Tip — The Hormozi Multiplication Method:**
Film ONE meat section, then record 3 different hooks for it. One shoot → 3 publishable Reels. This is how you scale from 3/week to 5/week without more filming.

### Reel Assembly Template

Every Reel follows this structure:

```
SECONDS 0-3: THE HOOK
├── Bold text overlay on screen (from Hook Bank)
├── Face in frame OR dramatic visual
├── Deliver the verbal hook line
└── Movement (walking, gesturing, or scene change)

SECONDS 3-20: THE MEAT
├── Use ONE of these formats:
│   ├── EDUCATION: Explain one concept clearly
│   ├── DEMONSTRATION: Show the experience (POV, walkthrough)
│   ├── TESTIMONIAL: Stack 2-3 guest clips or read reviews
│   ├── STORY: Tell one transformation narrative
│   └── FACELESS: Text on screen + B-roll footage
└── Keep ONE idea per Reel (don't try to cover everything)

SECONDS 20-30: THE REWARD + CTA
├── Deliver the insight/payoff ("The truth is...")
├── Soft CTA: "Save this" / "Share with someone who needs it"
└── OR Hard CTA: "Link in bio to start your journey"
```

## 4. Visual Standards

### Bold Text Overlay Style

Every Reel thumbnail needs bold text. This is non-negotiable — research shows 100% of top-performing Reels in this niche use them.

**Text Style:**
- Font: Bold sans-serif (Impact, Montserrat Black, or Instagram's built-in bold)
- Color: White with black outline/shadow (maximum contrast)
- Size: Large enough to read on a phone thumbnail (fill 40-60% of frame width)
- Position: Upper third of frame (so it's visible in the grid preview)
- Case: ALL CAPS for hook text
- Max words: 8-10 per overlay (any more = unreadable)

**Background:**
- Face visible behind or beside the text
- Nature/retreat setting visible
- Slight dim/gradient over footage if needed for text readability

### Thumbnail Formula

Every Reel thumbnail should have exactly 3 elements:
1. **Bold text** (the hook — from the overlay column in the Hook Bank)
2. **A face** (the creator, the shaman, or a guest)
3. **A setting** (jungle, ceremony space, or retreat grounds)

### Color Grading

Match the warm, authentic aesthetic of top-performing retreat content:
- **Primary:** Warm/golden tones (golden hour lighting preferred)
- **Secondary:** Rich greens (jungle, nature)
- **Avoid:** Cool/blue tones, heavy filters, overly polished corporate look
- **Editing:** Light color correction only — the goal is authentic, not cinematic

## 6. Awareness Level Content Mix

### How to distribute 3-4 weekly posts across the buyer journey

Most retreat accounts only post for people who already want ayahuasca. LaWayra's biggest growth opportunity is reaching people who don't know ayahuasca exists yet — the "Problem Aware" audience of millions struggling with depression, anxiety, trauma, and feeling stuck.

### Content Distribution Map

| Awareness Level | % of Posts | Posts/Month (at 4/week) | Description | Example Hook |
|-----------------|-----------|------------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Completely Unaware** | 10% | 1-2 | People don't know they have a problem ayahuasca solves | "The hidden pattern keeping high-achievers stuck" |
| **Problem Aware** | 30% | 4-5 | Know they're struggling, don't know ayahuasca is an option | "If therapy hasn't been enough, this is for you" |
| **Solution Aware** | 30% | 4-5 | Know about ayahuasca, exploring whether to try it | "This is what actually happens in a ceremony" |
| **Product Aware** | 20% | 3-4 | Know about retreats, comparing options | "Why 500+ guests chose LaWayra" |
| **Most Aware** | 10% | 1-2 | Ready to book, need the nudge | "March ceremony — 3 spots left. Apply now." |

### Weekly Awareness Level Rhythm

| Week Type | Post 1 | Post 2 | Post 3 | Post 4 |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Standard** | Problem Aware | Solution Aware | Product Aware | — |
| **Growth Push** | Unaware | Problem Aware | Solution Aware | Product Aware |
| **Conversion Push** | Solution Aware | Product Aware | Most Aware | Problem Aware |

**The 10:1 Give-to-Ask Ratio:**
For every 10 posts that give pure value (education, stories, proof), include 1 post with a direct booking CTA. At 4 posts/week, that's roughly 1 direct ask every 2.5 weeks. Everything else is building trust.

| **Follower Growth** | Audience building | +50/week | +100/week | +200/week |

| **Share Rate** | Viral potential indicator | Track only | 1%+ | 2%+ |
| **Posts Published** | Consistency check | 3/week | 4/week | 4-5/week |

### Monthly Review Process

At the end of each month:

1. **Identify top 3 posts** by views → What hooks/topics worked? Make more like these.
2. **Identify bottom 3 posts** by views → What fell flat? Make fewer like these.
3. **Check awareness level balance** → Are you over-indexing on one level?
4. **Review DM inquiries** → What questions are people asking? Turn those into content.
5. **Adjust hook bank** → Remove underperformers, add new hooks inspired by winners.

### The Hormozi Data Rule

> "Let the data do the teaching. Look at your top 10% of content. Make more like that. Look at the bottom 10%. Make less like that. Repeat until the end of time."
